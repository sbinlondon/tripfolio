what is tripfolio about?

Tripfolio aims to take the difficulty out of travel planning, whether it's a solo trip, you and your partner having a weekend getaway, or 25 friends going on a ski trip together.

For avid travellers, there are so many apps available to cover parts of trip planning. But where is the app that centralises everything in one place, making trip planning an easy, fun, collaborative experience? Tripfolio wants to be that app: storing your travel info, helping you find the best places to visit wherever you're going, making splitting travel costs easy, and much more.

about the cofounders

Kate and Joe met at Founders & Coders, the UK’s leading coding course.

Having gained a lot of experience across business development, marketing and ops, they both now are getting the vital programming skills that enable them to bring their ideas to life. Currently, both Kate and Joe are becoming proficient in everything involved in full stack development.

As with any good idea, Tripfolio comes out of a need to solve a problem both cofounders have faced and bonded over. Both are avid travellers, with Joe having spent the past year travelling the world (a large chunk of which involved living in a van with his dog!) and Kate having lived in the US, Munich, and London.

Kate has a varied background, having built a career as a freelance writer and photographer before deciding to join the fast-paced startup world at companies like tails.com and Monzo as an office manager. However, her need to create and innovate kept calling. This led her to leave her last job and take a chance on learning to code so she could channel her creativity into solving problems and building solutions through programming.

Joe's career has been spent bringing ideas to life. Having spent 5 years building Fun Kids into a national award-winning brand, he went on to run an influencer marketing company called Social Circle, innovating and overseeing the development of a specialist influencer tech platform. After setting up a new influencer agency for Good Relations, he quit his job to spend the year travelling while learning to code, experiencing first-hand the issues that we are now looking to solve at Tripfolio.
